## Executive Summary

The AI music generation market is rapidly maturing, with five major competitors — Suno, Udio, AIVA, Soundraw, and Boomy — establishing pricing norms between **$0 and $33/month**. This document analyzes the competitive landscape, identifies your unique advantages, and recommends a pricing structure with package tiers designed to maximize revenue while undercutting competitors on value.

Your application already has several differentiators that no competitor offers in a single platform: **professional sheet music generation with downloadable PDFs, acoustic guitar chord progressions with fingering diagrams, studio-grade audio post-processing with FFmpeg mastering presets, vocal-instrumental mixing, multiple vocal takes, and stem separation.** These features position Make Custom Music as the most complete creative toolkit in the market.

## Competitive Landscape

The table below summarizes the pricing and key differentiators of each major competitor as of early 2026.

| Competitor | Free Tier | Mid Tier | Top Tier | Unique Strength |
|------------|-----------|----------|----------|-----------------|
| **Suno** | $0 (10 songs/day) | $8/mo (500 songs/mo) | $24/mo (2,000 songs/mo) | Best overall quality (v5 model), personas, 12-stem split |
| **Udio** | $0 (10 credits/day) | $8/mo (2,400 credits/mo) | $24/mo (6,000 credits/mo) | Voice control, style reference, premium artist styles |
| **AIVA** | €0 (3 downloads/mo) | €11/mo (15 downloads/mo) | €33/mo (300 downloads/mo) | Classical/cinematic focus, MIDI export, orchestral |
| **Soundraw** | None | $11/mo (50 downloads/mo) | $32/mo (unlimited) | Royalty-free, customizable tempo/instruments |
| **Boomy** | $0 (limited) | $10/mo (unlimited saves) | $30/mo (25 downloads + streaming distribution) | One-click generation, Spotify/Apple Music distribution |

**Key market observations:**

The standard entry price for a paid AI music plan is **$8–$10/month**, with premium tiers clustering around **$24–$33/month**. Every competitor offers a free tier to drive adoption. Credit-based models (Suno, Udio) dominate over download-based models (AIVA, Soundraw) because they feel more generous and encourage experimentation. Commercial licensing rights are universally gated behind paid plans, which is the primary conversion driver from free to paid.

## Credit Cost Economics

Understanding the cost per generation is critical for setting sustainable pricing. Based on ElevenLabs API pricing:

| Action | Estimated API Cost | Credits Charged |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Generate song (instrumental) | ~$0.02–0.05 | 1 credit |
| Generate lyrics (Claude Sonnet) | ~$0.01 | Included with song |
| TTS vocal preview | ~$0.01–0.03 | 1 credit |
| Generate sheet music | ~$0.01 | Free (LLM call only) |
| Generate chord progression | ~$0.01 | Free (LLM call only) |
| Studio mastering (FFmpeg) | ~$0.00 (server CPU) | Free with paid plan |
| Multiple takes (3x vocal) | ~$0.03–0.09 | 3 credits |
| Vocal-instrumental mix | ~$0.00 (server CPU) | Free with paid plan |

**Gross margin analysis:** At the Creator tier ($9/mo for 100 songs), the estimated API cost is approximately $2–5/month per active user, yielding a **44–78% gross margin**. At the Professional tier ($22/mo for 500 songs), even with heavy usage the API cost is approximately $10–15/month, yielding a **32–55% gross margin**. The Studio tier at $49/mo has the best margins because most users won't fully utilize 2,000 credits.

## Competitive Features to Build

Based on the gap analysis, the following features should be prioritized to close the competitive gap and strengthen your market position.

### Critical (Build Immediately)

**1. Commercial Licensing System** — This is the single most important conversion driver. Every competitor gates commercial rights behind paid plans. Implement a licensing badge on each song that indicates the user's rights (personal only vs. commercial). Store the license type in the database tied to the user's plan at time of generation.

**2. Song Remix & Extend** — Allow users to take an existing song and extend it (add another verse/chorus), remix it (change the genre/mood while keeping lyrics), or create a cover (same lyrics, different voice). Suno and Udio both offer this and it dramatically increases engagement and retention.

**3. Usage Dashboard & Credit Tracker** — Display remaining credits, usage history, and generation analytics. This creates urgency to upgrade and helps users manage their allocation.

### High Priority (Build Within 30 Days)

**4. Audio Upload / Reference Track** — Let users upload a reference track to influence the style of generation. Both Suno and Udio offer this. It's a power-user feature that justifies the Professional tier.

**5. Custom Cover Art Generation** — Use the built-in image generation helper to create album art for each song. Udio offers this and it significantly improves the sharing experience.

**6. Song Sharing & Social Features** — Public song pages with embeddable players, social sharing buttons, and a community explore/discover feed. This drives organic growth.

### Medium Priority (Build Within 60 Days)

**7. Style Templates / Presets** — Pre-built genre+mood+voice combinations that users can select with one click (e.g., "Lo-Fi Study Beats," "Epic Cinematic Trailer," "Summer Pop Anthem"). Reduces friction for new users.

**8. Collaboration** — Allow users to share songs with collaborators who can edit lyrics, adjust mixing, or add their own vocal takes.

**9. Export to Streaming Platforms** — Partner with a distribution service (DistroKid, TuneCore) to let users push songs directly to Spotify, Apple Music, etc. Boomy's primary differentiator.

## Stripe Integration Architecture

Since you plan to integrate Stripe, the credit system should be designed with these Stripe primitives in mind:

**Products & Prices:** Create one Stripe Product per tier (Free, Creator, Professional, Studio) with two Prices each (monthly and annual). Credit packs are separate one-time Products.

**Subscriptions:** Use Stripe Subscriptions for recurring billing. On subscription creation/renewal, credit the user's account with the tier's monthly allocation.

**Webhooks:** Listen for `customer.subscription.created`, `customer.subscription.updated`, `customer.subscription.deleted`, and `invoice.payment_succeeded` to manage credit allocation and plan changes.

**Checkout:** Use Stripe Checkout Sessions for the initial purchase flow — this handles payment method collection, tax calculation, and SCA compliance automatically.

**Customer Portal:** Use Stripe's hosted Customer Portal for plan changes, cancellations, and payment method updates.

The database schema should include: `user_subscriptions` (plan, stripe_customer_id, stripe_subscription_id, status, current_period_end), `credit_balances` (user_id, monthly_credits, bonus_credits, purchased_credits), and `credit_transactions` (user_id, amount, type, description, created_at).
